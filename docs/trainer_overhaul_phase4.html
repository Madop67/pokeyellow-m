Pokémon Chromatic Yellow · Design Spec · Phase 4

Regular-Trainer Moveset Overhaul Every non-boss trainer in Kanto given a competitive, level-appropriate four-move set drawn from each Pokémon's own learnset and TM pool — walked route by route, gym junior to Victory Road.

00 Objective

Phases 1–3 rebuilt the thirteen bosses. Phase 4 finishes the job on the other ~380 fights: no more one-move Magikarps or double-Tackle filler — every regular trainer fields a deliberate set, while staying a clear tier below the gym leaders.

Four moves

Every Pokémon whose legal pool holds four moves gets four. Empty slots and dead weight are filled from its own learnset.

Boss-lite

STAB + coverage + up to one stat/status move — the boss doctrine, relaxed. No items, no roster changes.

Level-appropriate

A power ceiling scales with level, and the mon's natural level-up moves are strongly preferred over TMs, so early routes stay early.

Reproducible

All ~900 sets are generated from the data by a script and emitted as compact overrides — not hand-typed.

01 The moveset doctrine

"Boss-lite": competitive, but honest to the species. Applied per Pokémon over its legal pool — level-1 moves ∪ level-up moves ≤ its level ∪ TM/HM compatibility.

  1. STAB first, from the mon's own poolThe best same-type attack in the mon's stronger category (physical if Atk ≥ Sp. Atk, else special). Natural level-up moves win ties over TMs so sets stay thematic.
  2. Then coverageThe best off-type damaging move that widens what the mon threatens — a Rock Slide on a Machop, a Bubblebeam on a Goldeen.
  3. One utility slot, if it earns itA single meaningful stat/status move — Swords Dance, Agility, Screech, Sand-Attack, a sleep or paralysis move — but only when two damaging moves are already locked in. Weak −1 debuffs (Leer, Growl, Tail Whip) are dropped for damage.
  4. Fill to four, level-appropriatelyRemaining slots take the next-best capped move — even a modest one — before ever reaching past the level cap. A Lv 24 Sandshrew keeps Bulldoze; it does not get handed Fissure.
The power cap. Selectable move power is bounded by level — 60 through Lv 15, rising to 110 by Lv 46 — so the strongest 120-power moves only appear on the late-game trainers who have earned them. STAB is guaranteed even when a mon's only same-type option sits above the cap.
What regular trainers still don't get. Items, guaranteed six-mon teams, reassigned dual types, and fully hand-curated sets remain boss-only. Regular trainers keep their vanilla rosters and levels — only the four move slots change.

02 How it's built

Two moving parts: a generator that writes the data, and a bank move that makes room for it.

The generator

tools/gen_regular_movesets.py reuses the parsers behind the reference docs (move power/type/category, learnsets, TM pools). For each eligible Pokémon it runs the doctrine above, then writes data/trainers/special_moves.asm surgically — emitting only the slots that differ from the engine's default WriteMonMoves output, and keeping surviving default moves in place. The thirteen boss blocks and the two hand-authored vanilla-quirk blocks (Bug Catcher #15, Youngster #14) are preserved verbatim. Result: 309 trainer blocks, 1,557 override lines.

The bank relocation

A full moveset for every trainer blew the SpecialTrainerMoves table from a few hundred bytes to ~5.7 KB — far past the ~300 bytes free in the "Battle Engine 6" bank it lived in. The table is read same-bank by ReadTrainer, and a routine executing in the $4000–$7FFF window can't swap its own bank out, so the read had to move too.

engine/battle/read_trainer_party.asm — tail replaced with a far call
.AddAdditionalMoveData
-	ld a, [wTrainerClass]        ; ~90 bytes of table-walk,
-	...                         ; PP recompute, and prize-money
-	jr nz, .LastLoop            ; math inlined here
+	callfar ApplySpecialTrainerMoves
	ret

That self-contained tail — apply overrides, recompute PP, compute prize money — moved into ApplySpecialTrainerMoves in engine/battle/apply_special_moves.asm, co-located with the table in a new floating bank so the same-bank read still holds:

main.asm — new floating section
; The table floats freely; it's reached via BANK(ApplySpecialTrainerMoves).
SECTION "Trainer Special Moves", ROMX
INCLUDE "engine/battle/apply_special_moves.asm"
    ; ... which ends with INCLUDE "data/trainers/special_moves.asm"
Net effect. "Battle Engine 6" recovered ~1.5 KB of slack; the movesets got a fresh 16 KB bank (~5.7 KB used). The Phase-3 PP-recompute travels with the apply loop, so overrides written into empty slots stay usable instead of stuck at 0 PP.
