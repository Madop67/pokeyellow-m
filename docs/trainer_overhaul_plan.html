Pokémon Chromatic Yellow · Design Spec

Trainer Battle Overhaul Smarter items, competitive movesets, and rebuilt six-mon boss teams — every gym leader, Elite Four member, and the Champion given a dual-type identity while keeping their vanilla ace.

Touches: data/trainers/*, engine/battle/smart_ai.asm Build: plain make -j (no hash compare) Bosses: 13
Phase 1 · AI & items — done Phase 2 · rosters — written Phase 3 · boss movesets — written Phase 4 · 4-move sweep — deferred to own change

00 Objective

Make trainer fights feel deliberate instead of scripted — opponents that carry the right four moves, press advantages with items, and field bosses that read as designed teams.

Items

Trainers may use status heals & stat-boost items only — never HP recovery.

Movesets

Competitive sets drawn from each mon's legal learnset ∪ TM pool. Four moves whenever four are legal.

Boss teams

Six mons, no duplicate species, STAB + coverage + a stat move each.

Identity

Every boss gets a second type theme; ≥3 original mons kept; vanilla ace species preserved.

01 Global design rules

Four rules govern every change below. They are the contract the data has to satisfy.

  1. Items: status & stats, never HPBoss AI may cure a crippling status (Full Heal) and set up with X-items (X Attack/Defend/Speed/Special/Accuracy, Guard Spec., Dire Hit). All HP-restoring behavior — Potion, Super/Hyper Potion, Full Restore — is removed from the AI.
  2. Moves must be legal for the speciesA move is allowed only if it is in the mon's level-up list (learn level ≤ its level) or its TM/HM compatibility list. Level-up moves above the mon's level are not used; TM moves have no level gate (gym-leader convention). Every mon whose legal pool has ≥4 moves is given 4.
  3. Boss mons = STAB + coverage + statEach boss Pokémon carries its STAB attack(s), at least one coverage move against a common answer, and one stat-boosting or stat-lowering move (Swords Dance, Screech, Reflect, Thunder Wave, Leer, Growl, Double Team…).
  4. Boss compositionSix unique species · no repeats · a second type theme on the roster · keep ≥3 of the vanilla roster (or all, if it had fewer) · the highest-level mon stays the vanilla ace species.

02 AI & item logic

The fork already has a smart boss AI. The change is surgical: delete the HP-heal branch and retarget the status branch from Full Restore to Full Heal.

CommonBossAI in engine/battle/smart_ai.asm runs four steps: (1) cure sleep/freeze, (2) heal when below ⅓ HP, (3) open with an X-item, (4) switch out of a bad matchup. Steps 3–4 stay. Step 2 is deleted. Step 1 stops using Full Restore.

engine/battle/smart_ai.asm — CommonBossAI (before → after)
; 1) cure crippling status (sleep/freeze) immediately
    ld a, [wEnemyMonStatus]
    and SLP_MASK | (1 << FRZ)
    jr z, .checkXItem        ; was .checkHP
-   ld a, b
-   cp 2
-   jp z, AIUseFullRestore   ; HP+status — removed
+   jp AIUseFullHeal         ; status cure only, no HP
.checkHP  ; ---- ENTIRE STEP 2 DELETED ----
-   ld a, 3
-   call AICheckIfHPBelowFraction
-   jr nc, .checkXItem
-   ... AIUseSuperPotion / AIUseHyperPotion / AIUseFullRestore
.checkXItem   ; unchanged — open with class X-item
.checkSwitch  ; unchanged — switch on bad matchup

Then in engine/battle/trainer_ai.asm the per-class boss routines lose their now-meaningless heal-tier byte. The ld b, N line goes; the X-item pointer in hl stays and defines each boss's setup flavor.

Optional flavor: a few late-game regular classes (Cooltrainer M/F, Juggler, Blackbelt) already reach for X-items — that stays. No regular class needs HP items since none currently use them.

03 Moveset construction

Trainer mon moves come from two places; we drive both.

By default a trainer mon inherits the last four level-up moves it would know at its level (via AddPartyMon). data/trainers/special_moves.asm then overrides individual slots: db partyPosition, moveSlot, MOVE_ID. That override writes any move id regardless of level — the mechanism through which we assign TM moves and competitive picks.

The four-move rule

For every trainer mon, count the legal pool (level-up ≤ level ∪ TMs). If it is ≥4 but the default set left empty slots (common for early evolutions and mid-level mons), fill to four via special_moves.asm. Mons that genuinely cannot field four (Caterpie, Magikarp, a low-level Abra) are exempt — the rule is "can have four," not "must invent four."

Boss recipe (applied per mon)

SlotPurposeExample (Onix, Brock)
1Primary STABRock Rock Slide
2Secondary STAB / dual-type identityGrd Earthquake
3Coverage vs. a common answerStl Iron Head
4Stat move (boost/lower/control)Snd Screech ↓Def
Type theme via moves, not just bodies. The fork only has a handful of new-type species (Magneton = Electric/Steel, Wigglytuff = Sound/Fairy, Staryu/Starmie = Water/Light). Themes like Koga's Sound and Giovanni/Agatha's Dark are delivered mainly through retyped moves — Supersonic, Screech, Hyper Voice (Sound); Bite, Crunch, Dark Pulse (Dark) — which any compatible mon can carry. This matches the existing Koga set, which already runs Supersonic.

07 Implementation & verification

Phase 1
AI & itemsEdit engine/battle/smart_ai.asm (delete step 2, retarget step 1 → Full Heal) and engine/battle/trainer_ai.asm (drop heal-tier bytes). Smallest, highest-leverage change. Build & battle-test one boss.
Phase 2
Boss rostersRewrite the 13 boss entries in data/trainers/parties.asm to the six-mon lists above. Purely additive to $FF-format (per-mon level) entries.
Phase 3
Boss movesetsPopulate data/trainers/special_moves.asm with the full 4-move set for every boss mon per the STAB+coverage+stat recipe. Verify each move id against the mon's entry in docs/pokemon_reference.md.
Phase 4
Four-move sweep (regular trainers)Audit the ~180 non-boss parties; add special_moves overrides where a mon's default set left an empty slot but 4 legal moves exist. Optional/incremental — bosses already satisfy the brief without it.
Verify
Build & playtestmake -j$(nproc) — a clean assemble+link is the pass signal (hash compare is expected to fail in this fork). Then fight each rebuilt boss in the debug ROM to confirm rosters, movesets, item use, and no HP-heal behavior.

08 Open calls for you

Erika's ace is a vanilla tie. Weepinbell and Gloom are both Lv 32 in vanilla — no single "highest." This plan makes Gloom the Lv 34 ace (its Vileplume line is Erika's canonical signature). Say the word to make Weepinbell the ace instead.
Sabrina keeps a Lv 48 Abra. To honor "keep ≥3 originals," the whole Abra→Kadabra→Alakazam line is retained — but a high-level Abra is a weak lead with a shallow movepool. Alternative: keep only Kadabra + Alakazam and add a third distinct Psychic (e.g. Slowbro/Exeggutor), treating the single evolutionary line as fewer than three distinct picks.
Sound / Dark as move-flavor. Koga's Sound, Giovanni's & Agatha's Dark are delivered through moves, not a second type on the body, since no such species exist. If you'd rather see a literal Sound body on Koga, Wigglytuff (Sound/Fairy) is the only option — flag it and it goes in over Arbok.
Scope of Phase 4. The four-move sweep across ~180 regular parties is large and mechanical. Bosses meet the brief without it — decide whether to do it now, roll it out route-by-route, or skip.
